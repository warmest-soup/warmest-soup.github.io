     To live is to know the internal experience, or perhaps it's structure, which created all those who came before us, and all who will come after. The Human Experience is alike between individuals. Through the assumption of roles (or characters) we can learn to better understand ourselves, and our peers with whom we share this life, it's beauties, and it's sorrows.

This is the purpose behind RATTRAPS.

     RATTRAPS is a role play system designed with realism at the forefront. As such, all systems have been derived from their scientific and historical counterparts.
     In service of this, The Rule of Argument superceedes all others. This is the principal that; If an argument can be made sound, the action may be allowed. The G.M. will have final say in determining what constitutes a sound argument, but if, using logical principals and historical president, a directing player can satisfactorily articulate that a certain feat should be possible. This will override any rule herein.
The purpose of RATTRAPS is ultimately the simulation of reality, for the purpose of exploring characters. Thus the rules are always subordinate to the logic that was used to create them.

This rule also helps as a directing player's balance against the falible G.M. and helps to ensure a fair representation of their character.
"Nothing is impossible, not if you can dream it. That's what being a scientist is all about."
-Hubert J. Farnsworth
     RATTRAPS is most commonly played via correspondence, with each directing player in isolation from one another. this preserves anonymity, and allows players to immerse themselves fully in their character's thought process and decision making style absent the social pressures of a party, or group.
Betrayers betray, thieves steal, and villains must commit villainy. The only punishment to be faced for realizing your character well, are to be dealt unto the character in the form of reactions from the word and it's inhabitants, including other characters directed by other players. Communities are policed, murders are investigated, and crimes are punished via a judicial systems and avengers.
One should not hold back from themselves for the sake of others.

Players will take turns with their G.M., with the players directing their character's in response to the developments provided from the G.M..
Players will be assisted in tracking their character's needs and abilities using their character sheet.

This forms the basic structure of the game.

Below will be listed, in detail, all the terms, and mechanics that will be used in the course of a run.
-General-
Game Manager: The player responsible for rendering the game world, and directing NPCs. This player also will narrate the unconcious thoughts of player characters. These thoughts will never affect their decision making or personality, but will give their directing players a better understanding of their character's physical and mental state. For example the remnant feelings of pain from a past injury, or the sensations caused by failed checks are within the realm of the game manager's control, not the directing player.

Direction: You are not your character. in RATTRAPS characters have their own skillsets & abilities that they'll have to use in order to solve their problems. These abilities are often times unequal to the directing player's own faculties, thus it is better to approach your character not from the mindset of "controlling" them, but rather you are "directing" their actions.
They are their own person, and you are aiding them along, immersing yourself in their life.

Player: There exist two kinds of players in RATTRAPS; One is the G.M. while the others all direct their own individual characters. The generic term players most often refers to the latter kind.
It is the job of these players to immerse themselves in the role of their characters, and embody their personalities when directing their actions.
Players are not expected to cooperate, or ally themselves with one another. In fact this behavior is discouraged if it would compromise the character's own way of thinking.
To this end it is recommended that players maintain their anonymity during a run. Not knowing what player is directing what character helps to ensure authentic interactions and prevents interpersonal tensions. You are not your characters.

Character: Each player will create their own character. these can be anyone, accountants, adventurers, soldiers, vilains, royalty, anyone. These characters will be defined by their traits & abilities as well as by the personality that their directing player chooses to give them.
Players will direct their character until the run ends, or until the character retires, or dies.
These characters, and their interactions, are the backbone of RATTRAPS. It is through them that we can learn about ourselves, and the peers we share this life with.
Players will be aided in the managment of their characters by their character sheets, which contain all manner of helpful & necessary functions.

Non-Player Characters: It is the G.M.'s responsibility to fill the game world with interesting "NPCs" for the main cast to interact with. These will include the local citizenry, law enforcement, rivals, allies, enemys, and any other necessary persons to give the world it's depth.
During the character creation process, players may request that the G.M. direct NPC companions for their character. This can be a massive advantage, as well as a great enhancement to one's story through the run.
NPCs can also be generated at random through the Tarot system.

Cast: The cast refers collectively to all player characters. As these characters have the full creative attention of their directing players behind them, they will easily be the most interesting entities in any given run, as well as most likely to drive it's plot forward.

Character Sheet: A player's character sheet is their most important resource. It tracks experience, satiety, HP, statuses, inventory, time of day, all critical to the direction of one's character. Below is a list of sections that can be found on one's character sheet.

Run: RATTRAPS centers around the interconnected stories of it's Cast. There is no goal in RATTRAPS. Instead these stories and wander and weave in and out of eachother as each character pursues their own individual goals.
Runs are primarily defined by the creative team behind them. This includes the G.M. as well as all the involved players.
-Mechanics-

Checks: Whenever a character's abilities are tested, a check is performed. The player will roll a pair of standard seven-sum dice. If the lower of the die is equal to or less than the check's bar, then the check succeeds granting a favorable outcome to performing character.
If both dice are greater than the bar, then the check fails granting the character a negative outcome.
Should both rolled die show the same number (A pair), the result is considered critical.

The bar for checks can be raised by advantages and will be lowered penalties. Unfavorable rolls can be adjusted using the focus mechanic.

Some checks will ignore all penalties and advantages. these are referred to as flat checks.

50-50: A less common style of check, used primarily by the G.M., in which a singe dice is rolled with all odd numbers representing one option, and all even numbers representing a second.
These are most often used to make quick determinations in situations with two acceptable outcomes, and rarely will be used to make decisions of true importance.

Bar: The highest number a character can roll to pass an ability check. If the tested ability's rank is 3, the character must roll a 3 or below to pass.
The bar of checks is always modified by the total number of penalties & advantages applied to it.

Players will often need to estimate what the bar is for their checks based on the situation, and their character. one advantage will cancel out one penalty.
Advantages: Advantages are anything that makes an ability check easier for the performing character.
For example; were a character to attempt to leap across a gap in the floor, having footwear that grants extra traction would be an advantage in their favor. The floor being level and even, with no obstructions, would be another advantage.
Since each advantage raises the bar by 1 the hypothetical check above would have it's bar raised by 2, making it significantly easier to pass.

Advantages such as the ones used in the above example, that are derived from the current situation, are referred to as Contextual Advantages. These can be stacked with any incumbent advantages to greatly reduce the risk faced by the performing character, so be sure to always consider your situation carefully before rolling.

Every advantage on a passed check will decrease the exp earned for passing by 1.
In the event that an ability class or technique is used as an advantage in a check, experience lost will be awarded to the first class advantage noted.

Penalties: Penalties are anything that makes an ability check more difficult for the performing character.
For example; were a character to attempt reading a dense academic book, dim lighting would be a penalty. Were the character on a bumpy wagon ride, the erratic motions would be another penalty.
Since each penalty lowers the bar by 1 the hypothetical check above would have it's bar decreased by 2, making it significantly more difficult to pass.

Penalties such as the ones used in the above example, that are derived from the current situation, are referred to as Contextual Penalties. These will be stacked with any incumbent penalties to greatly increase the difficulty faced by the performing character. Be sure to always consider your situation carefully before rolling.

The G.M. will not inform players of contextual penalties when they are performing a check.
Always exersize your own caution when directing characters.

Every penalty on a passed check will increase the exp earned for passing by 1.

Incumbents: Any trait or ability that's rank is not 3, has incumbent advantages if it's higher, and incumbent penalties if it's lower.

Rank 1: 2 penalties
Rank 2: 1 penalties.
  ⭡     ⭡     ⭡
Rank 3: No inbumbents.
  ⭣     ⭣     ⭣
Rank 4: 1 advantages.
Rank 5: 2 advantages.


These are representative of your mastery (Or lack thereof) of the ability in question.
Incumbent advantages & penalties will still modify the experience earned from checks. Meaning it's more difficult to earn experience for high rank abilities, as they require more difficult checks to earn experience.

Incumbents are lost and earned naturally as a character progresses through ranks. all abilities are affected by incumbents.

Quirks: During character creation, when setting their trait ranks, Players can choose to replace incumbent advantages or penalties with positive or negative quirks related to the trait in question.
Penalties must be replaced with negative quirks, while penalties must be replaced with positive quirks.
Quirks are in essence, niche advantages & penalties, that are unlikely to affect all your checks, but due to their nature may affect checks for more than just the trait they're under.
For example; A character might choose arachnophobia as a Wisdom Quirk. They will no longer need to overcome their incumbent Penalty, but will have to deal with more severe penalties when around spiders, or things that remind them of spiders.

Quirks can be used to turn Penalties into advantages through their unintended side effects. Another example; A character might choose to take paranoia as a negative Perception Quirk. This might cause unnecesary checks, but would also grant advantages in the event of a real threat. More frequent checks would also lead to more experience gained.

Unlike incumbents, Quirks do not dissapear as one raises their trait ranks. when an incumbent penalty is replaced with a negative quirk, that quirk is kept indefinitely. Whenever a character raises a Quirked trait rank, the directing player may choose not to raise their rank in order to remove their negative quirk.

Quirks can also be gained or lost rarely through developments in a character's personal story.

Seven-Sum Dice: RATTRAPS is played using two standard Seven-Sum Dice. This means that oppopsite faces will always add up to seven. (Five is opposite two, six is opposite one.)

Focus: Character's can modify any dice roll using the relavant trait. Dex is used to focus skill rolls, while Wisdom is used to focus insight rolls.

When focusing a roll, the either dice maybe raised or lowered by one, as many times as the performing character has ranks in the relavant trait.

Focusing a roll costs one card of relavant type.

Rank: A character's talent in any given trait or class. Ranks determine the natural bar of checks which become easier as Ranks increase, as well as that ability's incumbents.
Trait Ranks also determine a character's access to quirks while technique class ranks often provide clas bonuses that grow in effectiveness with higher ranks.

Traits: The fundemental abilities that define a character. Traits are generic, and widely applicable abilities. Like all abilities, they recieve incumbents, but unlike skills & insights, the incumbents of traits can be traded for Quirks to further specialize a character.

Athleticism: A category of traits that, together, offer an idea of a character's apptitude for athletics. these are the primary traits used in combat, but affect all physical fields as well.
Athleticicsm includes Speed, Strength, and Dexterity.

Strength: A measure of a character's ability to move and carry weight. Strength determines how much of a load they can carry, what objects they can lift, and what weapons they have difficulty wielding.
Strength is greatly beneficial in all manners of work and labor, as well as in grappling exchanges, where strength is added to their grapple check, making foes easier to deal with.

Dexterity: A measure of a character's fine motor skills. Dexterity determines they effectiveness with edged weapons, how likely they are to be disarmed, as well as how many actions they're able to perform in one round of combat.
Dexterity is extremely useful for finer actions, such as playing instruments, writing, and Precision striking.

Dexterity is also the trait used to focus skill rolls and checks.

Speed: A measure of a character's gross motor skills anad coordination. Speed determines they melee damage, How likely they are to recover from being destablized, their resistance to being tripped, as well as how many actionable moments they have in every round of combat.
When it comes to combat, Speed is the most important trait.

Athleticism and it's subordinate traits are considered to be skills.
Awareness: A category of traits that, together, offer an idea of a character's understanding of the things around them. these are the primary traits used in exploration, and social situations.
Awareness includes Perception, Navigation, and Charisma.

Awareness and it's subordinate traits are considered to be insights.

Perception: A measure of a character's situational awareness and the acuity of their sense. Perception determines how much detail they can percieve in their enviornment, how easy it is to visually target objects & entities with ranged weapons & magic, and how much of their battle ticket *must* be turned in at a time.
For cautious characters, or those interested in gathering detail from the enviornment, perception is very important.

Navigation: A measure of a character's spacial awareness and their ease of orienting themselves in space. Navigation determine's a character's ability to avoid getting lost or turned around, and is very important when traveling often or navigating dense interior spaces.

Charisma: A measure of a character's social aweareness, and their ability to understand the social ques & meanings behind the words of others. For social characters, charisma is the most important trait as it allows a greater understanding of the emotional states & motivations of the character's one interacts with.

Body: A category of traits that, together, offer an idea of a character's physical hartiness. These are the primary traits used in dertemining one's physical wellness, and one's natural resistances.
Body includes Endurance, Constitution, and Stamina.

Body and it's subordinate traits are considered to be Skills

Stamina: A measure of a character's physical fitness, and how long they can remain active. Stamina primarily determines the maximum number of skill cards a character has accsess to, and thus, how often they can use techniques or focus.

Endurance: A measure of a character's physical Toughnesss, and how difficult they are to injure. Determines a character's natural DT, as well as their pain tolerance.

Constitution: A measure of the efficiency with wich a character's body operates, and how difficult it is to disturb those operations. Constitution determines a character's natural damage reduction, their DP.
